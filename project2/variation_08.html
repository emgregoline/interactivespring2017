Girl
Jamaica Kincaid

  1. Wash the white clothes on Monday and put them on the stone heap;
  2. wash the color clothes on Tuesday and put them on the clothesline to dry;
  3. don’t walk bare-head in the hot sun;
  4. cook pumpkin fritters in very hot sweet oil;
  5. soak your little cloths right after you take them off;
  6. when buying cotton to make yourself a nice blouse, be sure that it doesn’t have gum in it, because that way it won’t hold up well after a wash;
  7. soak salt fish overnight before you cook it;
  8. is it true that you sing benna in Sunday school?;
  9. always eat your food in such a way that it won’t turn someone else’s stomach;
  10. on Sundays try to walk like a lady and not like the slut you are so bent on becoming;
  11. don’t sing benna in Sunday school;
  12. you mustn’t speak to wharf-rat boys, not even to give directions;
  13. don’t eat fruits on the street—flies will follow you;
  14. but I don’t sing benna on Sundays at all and never in Sunday school;
  15. this is how to sew on a button;
  16. this is how to make a buttonhole for the button you have just sewed on;
  17. this is how to hem a dress when you see the hem coming down and so to prevent yourself from looking like the slut I know you are so bent on becoming;
  18. this is how you iron your father’s khaki shirt so that it doesn’t have a crease;
  19. this is how you iron your father’s khaki pants so that they don’t have a crease;
  20. this is how you grow okra—far from the house, because okra tree harbors red ants;
  21. when you are growing dasheen, make sure it gets plenty of water or else it makes your throat itch when you are eating it;
  22. this is how you sweep a corner;
  23. this is how you sweep a whole house;
  24. this is how you sweep a yard;
  25. this is how you smile to someone you don’t like too much;
  26. this is how you smile to someone you don’t like at all;
  27. this is how you smile to someone you like completely;
  28. this is how you set a table for tea;
  29. this is how you set a table for dinner;
  30. this is how you set a table for dinner with an important guest;
  31. this is how you set a table for lunch;
  32. this is how you set a table for breakfast;
  33. this is how to behave in the presence of men who don’t know you very well, and this way they won’t recognize immediately the slut I have warned you against becoming;
  34. be sure to wash every day, even if it is with your own spit;
  35. don’t squat down to play marbles—you are not a boy, you know;
  36. don’t pick people’s flowers—you might catch something;
  37. don’t throw stones at blackbirds, because it might not be a blackbird at all;
  38. this is how to make a bread pudding;
  39. this is how to make doukona;
  40. this is how to make pepper pot; this is how to make a good medicine for a cold;
  41. this is how to make a good medicine to throw away a child before it even becomes a child;
  42. this is how to catch a fish;
  43. this is how to throw back a fish you don’t like, and that way something bad won’t fall on you;
  44. this is how to bully a man;
  45. this is how a man bullies you;
  46. this is how to love a man, and if this doesn’t work there are other ways, and if they don’t work don’t feel too bad about giving up;
  47. this is how to spit up in the air if you feel like it, and this is how to move quick so that it doesn’t fall on you;
  48. this is how to make ends meet;
  49. always squeeze bread to make sure it’s fresh;
  50. but what if the baker won’t let me feel the bread?;
  51. you mean to say that after all you are really going to be the kind of woman who the baker won’t let near the bread?
